Privacy Policy

Last updated: 1 July 2026

1. Our Approach

Capstone Labs (working name: Die Zukunftswerkstatt) respects your privacy. This policy explains how we handle any personal information collected through this website. Our ABN will be added upon registration.

2. What We Collect

Our website is primarily informational. We only collect personal information when you voluntarily provide it to us through our contact form. This may include:

  • Name
  • Email address
  • Organisation name
  • Any details you include in your message

3. How We Use Your Information

Information submitted through our contact form is used solely to respond to your enquiry and to discuss potential services. We do not use your contact details for marketing unless you have expressly consented. We do not sell, rent, or trade personal information to third parties.

4. Third-Party Services

Our contact form is processed by Formspree (or an equivalent third-party form handling service). When you submit the form, your data is transmitted to that service for delivery to our team. Their privacy practices are available on their respective website. We do not use analytics trackers, advertising cookies, or any other tracking technology on this site.

5. Data Storage & Security

We take reasonable steps to protect your information from misuse, loss, and unauthorised access. Contact form submissions are handled in accordance with our form processor's data retention policies. For engagements that proceed, data handling is governed by our client engagement agreements, which include confidentiality and data-protection provisions.

6. Your Rights

Under Australian privacy law (Privacy Act 1988), you have the right to:

  • Request access to personal information we hold about you
  • Request correction of inaccurate information
  • Request deletion of your personal information
  • Make a complaint about how we have handled your information

To exercise these rights, contact us using the details in section 8.

7. Notifiable Data Breaches

In the event of a data breach likely to result in serious harm, we will notify affected individuals and the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) in accordance with the Notifiable Data Breaches (NDB) scheme under Part IIIC of the Privacy Act 1988.

8. Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy from time to time. The latest version will always be available on this page. We encourage you to review it periodically.
